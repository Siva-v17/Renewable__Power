Quick Answers

Thinking About Going Solar?

Most homes offset 70–90% of their electricity bill. A typical 5 kW system in Tamil Nadu generates around 600 kWh a month — at ₹8/kWh that's roughly ₹4,800 of grid power you no longer buy. Use the calculator above for a quick estimate.

Most concrete, metal-sheet, and tiled roofs work. What matters is shade-free area (about 80 sq ft per kW), structural condition, and orientation — south-facing is ideal but east/west works well too. Our free drone survey settles it in one visit.

Under the national rooftop solar programme, residential customers can claim a central subsidy of up to ₹78,000 depending on system size. We file the application, upload documents, and track the disbursement for you at no extra charge.

Quality panels are warrantied for 25 years and typically keep producing beyond 30. Output declines slowly — about 0.5% per year — so a panel still delivers ~85% of its original power at year 25. Inverters are usually replaced once in that lifetime.

At night your home draws from the grid (or your battery, if installed). On cloudy days panels still generate 10–30% of peak output. With net-metering, surplus daytime energy you export offsets what you import later — your bill nets out.
