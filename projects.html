Case Studies

Three Projects. Three Very Different Challenges.

Engineering problems are rarely the same twice. Here's how we solved three of the harder ones.

Case Study 01 · Floating Solar

Mettur Reservoir — 12 MW Floating Array

The challenge: Installing 32,000 modules on a live water body used for irrigation release, with no land access, monsoon windows only 11 weeks wide, and TANGEDCO interconnection at 2 km away.

Our solution: HDPE floating pontoons anchored to a custom mooring grid, barge-mounted cranes, and a pre-built modular string design that cut on-water assembly time by 60%. Grid connection via underwater armoured cable to a dedicated pooling substation.

Case Study 02 · Off-Grid

48 Villages — Microgrid Without the Grid

The challenge: Forty-eight hamlets in the Nilgiris with no grid connection, diesel generators running at ₹22/unit, and roads impassable by heavy equipment for six months of the year.

Our solution: Containerised 1.2 MW solar + 2 MWh LFP microgrid per cluster, helicopter-lifted to four of the most remote sites, pre-wired and plug-and-play. Local youth trained as certified operators within 90 days of commissioning.

Case Study 03 · Agrivoltaic

Erode Agrivoltaic Canopy — Land That Does Double Duty

The challenge: A cooperative of 220 turmeric farmers needed power for cold storage and pump sets, but couldn't afford to give up a single acre of productive farmland for a conventional ground-mount array.

Our solution: Elevated bifacial modules at 4 m height — enough clearance for tractor access below — oriented east-west to reduce peak irradiance and extend the morning and evening harvest windows. Crop yield in the shaded micro-climate actually increased 12% in year one due to reduced heat stress.
